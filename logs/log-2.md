# Log 02 - May 13, 2026 (Buhay na!)

Short log today. But an important one.

The PSU arrived. A Powerlogic ATX-550-W, generic, budget, Made in China. On paper it claims 550 watts. In reality it probably delivers a honest 350 to 400 watts continuous. None of that matters for this build tho, because the HP 8200 at full load draws around 95 watts (approximately). This PSU is running at less than a third of its real capacity. It will be fine.

Before connecting anything to the motherboard, the paperclip test came first. Bent a paperclip into a U shape, bridged the green PS-ON wire to a black ground wire on the 24-pin connector, plugged it into the wall. The fan spun up immediately. Quiet too. No coil whine, no struggling, no burning smell. Just a clean, quiet spin.

Buhay na!!!

That is the first moment in this build where electricity actually moved through something intentionally. Everything before this was planning, measuring, and ordering. This was the first real confirmation that the power chain is alive and ready to feed the board.

The voltage rails were also checked with a clamp meter on DC voltage mode. The 12V yellow rail, the 5V red rail, and the 3.3V orange rail all read within acceptable range. Nothing sagging, nothing fluctuating. Stable and clean at idle with no load attached.

The harness also got ordered today. An ATX 24-pin to HP 6-pin conversion cable, explicitly listed as compatible with the HP Elite 8100, 8200, 8300, and 800G1. 18AWG tinned copper wire, 30cm length. That cable is the last critical missing link between the PSU and the motherboard. Once it arrives and the standoffs are confirmed to fit the board holes, the first POST attempt becomes possible.

The PSU dimensions are also now confirmed and written into the build plan. 15 by 15 centimeters footprint, 8 centimeters tall. The bottom shelf of the Bakal-Gubat frame needs to accommodate at least 19 by 19 centimeters of clear floor space for the PSU with airflow clearance on all sides. The fan intake faces down so a cutout or raised mounting will be needed under the unit so it is not suffocating against solid wood.

One component at a time. One test at a time. The build is moving...
