# Bakal-Gubat

A custom open-air wood PC build using a salvaged HP Compaq 8200 motherboard, threaded rod columns, and dark hardwood shelving. This is not just a PC build, but a personal engineering project, a learning journal, and also a statement that old hardware deserves a second life:>

The name means **Steel Forest** in Filipino/Tagalog. The metal holds it together. The wood gives it a soul.

---

## What this is

Bakal-Gubat started as an attempt to turn an old HP Compaq 8200 SFF into a personal cyberdeck. That plan evolved. The cyberdeck is on hold. What remained was the motherboard, the parts, the wood, and also the plan to build something that looks like it grew out of the ground and runs on electricity.

The final form is an open-air threaded rod PC tower with dark wood shelves, exposed components, a custom IO panel, and a forest aesthetic with vines and dark stain. Built by hand. Documented honestly, including the mistakes.

## Build Philosophy

This rig is old by modern standards. The CPU is from 2011 for goodness sake! The motherboard is proprietary and very VERY stubborn. None of that matters.

The goal here is not to build the fastest PC, or the most powerful rig. The goal is to build something with your own hands, and understand every part of it, document the process, and end up with a machine that works and looks like nothing else on the desk.

If you are reading this and you have old hardware sitting in a corner, this is proof that it does not have to stay there.
